Research Statement

My research focuses on robust, human-centered embodied AI for real-world robotics systems operating in complex industrial and logistics environments.

Research Vision

I aim to build autonomous systems that combine strong technical performance with transparency, safety, and practical deployability. In safety-critical settings, successful autonomy requires reliable perception, resilient localization, explainable decision pathways, and effective human supervision interfaces.

Core Research Themes

  • Multi-modal perception using camera, LiDAR, and sensor fusion for robust scene understanding.
  • Mission-level autonomy with explicit task contracts, runtime observability, and graceful failure handling.
  • Human-in-the-loop autonomy for trustworthy handoff between autonomous and teleoperated modes.
  • Cross-platform transfer of autonomy stacks across mobile robots, quadrupeds, and industrial vehicles.

Applied Impact

My work is grounded in field deployment. I prioritize methods that remain reliable under environmental variability, compute constraints, and operational uncertainty. The objective is to move beyond prototype performance toward dependable real-world autonomy that can scale across sites and use cases.
