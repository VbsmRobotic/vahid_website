LiDAR + Vision in the Field

Multi-modal perception succeeds when sensing, timing, and runtime constraints are designed as one system.

Camera and LiDAR fusion improves scene understanding, but only when calibration and time synchronization are continuously maintained in operations. Weather, vibration, and clutter quickly expose weak assumptions.

Practical deployments prioritize robust defaults, bounded compute latency, and clear quality metrics over algorithmic complexity alone. This keeps output predictable for planning and control.
